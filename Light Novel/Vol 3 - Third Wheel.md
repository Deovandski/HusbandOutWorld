Sanbi Kingdom’s “economical department” was renowned for its extensive collection of tax documents and reports on cost-saving measures, a beacon of frugality for all to see\! Contrasting such boring-ass documents, a wall of mannequins with maidly clothing that sometimes looked skimpy, sometimes cute, sometimes a bit of both matched the portraits of their famous wearers. And don't forget the thick metal door leading to the coffers, of which only the Sanbi royalty with their painted blue hair strands knew what was inside\! Maybe the "True Sanbi King" would know it too—or not\! The "true king," revered by the Sanbi people, was a humongous fish that salivated over old people's skin, allowing Sanbi fishermen to catch its younglings in exchange for raisin-looking leather treats. All maidly outfits, cute or sexy alike, revered this “true king” in some way. Such a distinct place brimming with those outfits and documents was located on the second floor of the Sanbi castle, not so coincidentally, where the King-Maid Eiri called her happy place\! The only place where she truly had control\! Everything was spotlessly clean and arranged in a way that tickled her brain the most. 

However, such a happy place was once again invaded by a creature with an aura that spoiled the air itself, or perhaps Eiri associated rose water mixed with wine notes perfume oozing from this tainted creature with negative thoughts. A mental *Hmmmm* rattling inside Eiri’s skull slipped out into a grumbled “*Hmmmmm*” as the creature drew closer with a familiar-looking white envelope.

“Your dearly Sanbi-eye is taking steps to fulfill his responsibilities effectively\!” The creature everyone knew as the Queen of Sanbi, Neferteri Sanbi Royal, spoke in an unusual, delightful tone, contrasting her usually neutral tone, which made understanding what’s inside her skull a black void of motivations and feelings—except this time.

	Eiri grabbed the letter, feeling Neferteri’s thumb still slightly gripping in. *Sanbi Mail* was written on the same face where a broken blue wax stamp once protected the contents of this undetermined letter. Feeling a curiosity bubbling inside, Eiri hastily took out the letter from within, focusing on every word with enough attention as the letter’s handwriting was hard to read, either looking outright sloppily written, or the person had some sort of muscle coordination issues.

*Dear Sanbi Queen Neferteri,*

*Sorry for writing to Eiri before you and for not being as clear as I should have been in the letters. I will try my best to give more details on all the people’s names and descriptions from now on\! Sorry for not knowing that people outside the kingdom rarely have badges and need more than just the name.*

Almost forgot\! ~~Tupima~~ Tupinamba looked old and cranky, but he seemed to speak truth with people in the room, really, really agreeing with him when he talked about the smith. For the villagers, they all seem to agree with the chief, and they look happy too\! Going to spend another day ~~mim~~ having fun with them\! They loved the presence of a kingdom official\!*

*Harry, the Sanbi-eye of the great Sanbi Kingdom\!*

Paulin 47th, Tollday. “So what did you write to him? He hasn’t said anything to me since the Sanbi mail you brought a few weeks ago—” Eiri’s tone grew slightly sarcastic as her eyes narrowed on Neferteri, folding the letter back into the envelope. “Which, of course, you would know that already\!”

	“Indeed, I am well aware.” Neferteri slowly nodded. “The contents of what I stated to him are not yours to know; it’s the Monarchy department affairs.” Her delightful tone waned back to cold neutrality.

	“But—but I’m the King Maid\!” With what appeared to be the last spark of fire in her spirit, Eiri stood up and moved her hand to show the room, “I’m the economical department\!” She seemed to look for words for a second, leaving a cringe-inducing air to fill the room.

	“If you—” Neferteri began to fill the void with her carefully selected words. Her eyes still looked down towards Eiri, even though there wasn’t much height difference between them.

	“No\! I’m not finished\!” Eiri pointed at Neferteri with her gloved yellow hand, “I—I need to know what the Monarchy is spending its resources on\! Yes\! I—”

	“—I need to know that so that I can better advise—” Eiri’s babble began to lose steam again, her mind searching for something snarky and well-said to front the one and only Queen of Sanbi. “That’s it\!” A thought slipped from her mouth, “This whole Harry thing was costed by you only\! You did not even give me a choice to review it\! You didn’t talk about the Sanbi-eye thing until Harry already had shown up\!”

Why must you fail as a King-maid not to have questioned such presumed audacity in the first meeting concerning this subject back on Paulin 11th?”

	Feeling a chill coming down her spine, Eiri's mouth began to betray her, “But-did—I did\!”

	With a simple, slow shake of the head, Neferteri shut up the fumble. “You did not make any objections to the request my department proposed. We stated clearly that we desired one sapphire and five aquamarines to invest in finding a potential husband for Maiara\! Once we had found the potential partner, we would alter him with the soul energy department's assistance according to her wishes for a public display of love through a Gandew duel.”

	“Furthermore\!” Neferteri spoke neutrally, but her volume went up by a notch before returning to normal. “Harry’s servitude to our great Sanbi Kingdom for losing the Gandew duel must trump any of your feelings for him. He was supposed to be Maiara’s husband, not yours.” With a smooth drag, Neferteri pulled the white envelope on the table away from Eiri, picking it up gently, “This simple matter is not even why I choose to invest my time to walk over and listen to your unnecessary pleading\! We have a greater concern\!”

	*What in the Sanbi King is happening—* Eiri widened her eyes as her stance waned. Her brain tried to foresee and think ahead, but all it did was fire randomly aimlessly.

	“—you unfortunately know it as Gabiro.” In this silent room, Neferteri’s voice felt like it was echoing off the walls, repeating the monotone words over and over.

Sitting down and shoving cocona with cadioca biscuits in her mouth, Eiri tried to focus instead on the starchy, earthy, slightly sweet flavors of the cadioca in the white paste, contrasting the tart, semi-salty glaze of the cocona in the red cream. 

	“Good.” Neferteri kept staring at Eiri. “There will be a shoutcast today to gather citizens on the Sunnilow of Paulin 50th at the Old Wharf.”

Eiri kept eating the sweet and savory biscuit with distant eyes but still listened to every word from Neferteri. She could imagine a public announcement of Gabiro’s death. With a loud crunch, she stopped as a precise memory came to mind, “Wait a hunk\! Our official—” making quotation marks with her hand, Eiri continued with a blunter tone, “message is that Gabiro was recovering from a lab accident, so what—oh” She took another bite of the biscuit, chewing it at the same sluggish pace her dozen thoughts chewed at the back of her mind.

 Neferteri’s voice kept clashing with the crunchy outer shell of the biscuits Eiri was eating, “If by that *oh* you meant Kibe being inept at restoring Gabiro, then indeed that is why we must be transparent to our great kingdom citizens. Gabiro has soul died. Not only so, Kibe will accuse Gabiro of tampering with soul research without consent from the other departments.”

The sound of biscuit chewing stopped. Leaving a growing, dead quiet in the room that consumed whatever was left of the tranquility Eiri craved. *Oh no, he’s fucked\!* She could almost feel a bead of cold sweat running down her check.

“Your directive is simple—do not clash with Kibe’s statement, and burn all evidence that says the opposite.” Neferteri calmly laid down the white envelope back on the reddish wooden table, putting it closer to Eiri than her. “Otherwise, I shall see that your guild becomes nothing more than a word in a forgotten book.” With an elegant turn and steady walk, she paced out of the room, each clack echoing through the silence.

	*But—but\!* Eiri's mouth wanted to speak, but nothing was immediately forming as she saw the envelope patiently waiting to be picked up. With a sudden long breath, her feelings spilled out, “What will happen to Kibe?”

	With a short stop, Neferteri did not look back. “He will become the next courtroom scientist, as per tradition.” She continued going out the door, “Be better than your infamous mother\!” With a soft yet firm shut of the door, her walking could still be softly heard echoing outside.

With a long sigh, Eiri felt the tranquility slowly return to her happy place. Glancing sharply at the sassy portrait of a familiar person, she confronted it with a single question: “What am I supposed to do now?” 

Removing her gloves, Eiri began to chew on her short nails as dozens of thoughts churned in the back of her mind all fought for processing time. *Gabiro was in his own world most of the time, but it’s wrong to blame him for everything—hmmm—the excuse is pretty airtight, though.*

*What a bitch—but she actually thought of everything\!* Eiri caught herself and slipped her hand back into the yellow glove, grabbing the white envelope and putting it between her and the sassy-looking figure in the portrait, smirking back at her. “AH\! Leave me alone\!”

The long-dreaded morning of Paulin 50th had arrived. *Why do they keep doing this kind of shit on Forrdays? That’s just wasting everyone’s free day\!* A single thought passed on Kibe’s mind as his eyes avoided the murmuring crowd and craved the serenity of his office solitude, where he was least alone. A continuous clench in Kibe’s chest must be because he was in a large crowd\! A pit in his stomach must have been because of his meh tasting food that Arukia brought him for breakfast, and her diabetic-inducing perfume lingered in the room when she took her sweet time to leave him alone. *At least she wasn’t bitching this time.* Kibe thought painedly as the sickly-sweet smell returned to his mind, making the pit in his stomach grow. His swollen voicebox was getting itchy, and the back of his throat was getting drowned in spit, but he avoided clearing his throat as there was no escape from that feeling while under the Darfaries interface effects to raise his voice. Amidst these random thoughts, explaining away this shitty situation he was in, one thing appeared to be in common that wasn’t being addressed—likely on purpose. 

The crowd was still gathering people, with most people walking towards the congregation without a single worry in their step. Amidst the beached boat showing a portrait of Gabiro, a deep-sea-looking amulet, and his paraphernalia, stood a jar containing some weird fleshy thing that gave the crowd an icky feeling—whatever that was, it did not seem human. But that wasn’t the only thing competing for people’s curiosity\! Some people in front were fixated on the two mysterious-looking boxes, which emitted a faint white light from their joint gaps. They were perfectly hot-branded with the Sanbi logo and PROPERTY OF SANBI KINGDOM text on it.

Kibe’s ears kept avoiding hearing the unnaturally clamorous near shoutings of a friendly with a hint of authoritative speech from the one and only King of Sanbi. However, to no avail, a trigger word forced his speech way into his mind.

“—about Kibe? Yeah. We will talk about it shortly, but first, we gotta do the soul sendaway\!” Itagiba’s voice did not sound strained despite echoing outwardly like it was spoken in a megaphone. His hand holding his blacker badge high up in the air showed a faint red glow from the thermometer-like apparatus. Shuffling his hand up in the air to further call attention to himself, he looked away from the front row and focused into the sea of eyes aimed towards him, “Okay—look\! We know quite a few of you were not Gabiro fans, but he was the courtroom scientist of Sanbi\! We gotta pay our respects\! We also have some serious stuff to talk about, so don’t crack the booze yet\!” With a quick look up and a silent nod towards the lone man in a boat connected to the beached boat, Itagiba spoke even louder, “Let’s officially start Gabiro’s soul sendaway\!”

Applauses came lethargically from the crowd. Although, some did not applaud how one would imagine. Instead, with their hands extended outwardly pointed at the old man portrait, they clapped their hand upwardly, like an eating motion—which meant one, and only one thing in Sanbi culture: get fucked\! Meanwhile, the lone guy in the boat chucked one deep blue colored ball after the other into the sea, then dusted his hands off. 

Those balls made a nice-sounding plunk in the water, and soon after, a colorful, deep blue smoke began to bubble.

“A chunk of you were born and grew up knowing Gabiro as the courtroom scientist, so this one is tailor-made for you\!” Itagiba chortled loudly as the wrinkles on his face showed he had much to tell. “I’m going to date myself here by saying this happened twenty-four years ago, but here we go\! I still remember one of my first weeks as King, and I requested Nuru to replace his apprentice with Gabiro.” with a heavy nod and a wrinkled face, Itagiba continued, “They were definitely not into him when I mentioned he was eight-something—how old was he again?” He tilted towards his wife, the Queen of Sanbi.

 	“Seventy-eight,” with a precise answer, Neferteri responded with a curt smile, presumptively hiding away her sadness. Her voice had the same loudness as her husband, yet sounded far more silvery than impactful.

	“Right\! Pretty damm old, but there’s a reason for it\! Y’all remember Gydy beer?”

	The crowd barely reacted, with some sparse hands raised in the air. An interesting correlation is that the more rugged and older a person's skin was, the more likely they were to raise their hand.

	“EXACTLY\! It was famous, and pretty damm tasty too\! But somehow went to shit\! Guess who worked there? This fucking guy\!” Itagiba pointed at the painted picture of Gabiro, perfectly capturing his exuding boisterous mixed with assholish vibes. “And no\! He was the researcher, and that beer came out perfect from every keg I drank\!” With his free hand, Itagiba pounded his chest, “And I made sure to test out a bunch of them\! Hehe—it was so damm good I just wanted to grab a drink with whoever created my favorite beer—” With a loud smirky tone, Itagiba tried to contain his mischievous laughter as he kept on, “—just maybe get in their good graces to get a factory here\!”

One that I’m truly thankful to Nefi\!” Itagiba hugged his wife from the side, lowering his badge.

He invented it and then got Forway to give the comparative advantage to our kingdom\! If you see that same smoke somewhere else, it came from Sanbi\! It came from Ungady’s factory\!” She pointed to a street going to her right away from the Old Wharf.

*That’s because he had to do it\! He hated it\!* Kibe felt a sliver of wanting to say something, but it soon died thinking of how much of a hassle this whole thing already was. His eyes kept on avoiding the crowd as another thought passed through his bored mind. *Can we just get to the accusation part already?*

Meanwhile, the crowd energy began deflating. Maybe it was the introspective nature of seeing Gabiro’s achievements impacting people’s daily lives, Eiri’s subdued speech or people were craving to know what the gossip around town was about the fanchaki incident rather than the goodbye party of an old, unlikable smarty-pants. Meanwhile, With a soft tug on Itagiba’s white cape, the girl in pink, known as none other than Nina Sanbi Royal Fourth-Princess, silently called out to her father with tears slowly pouring down her cheeks.

	Emelia kept silent, tilting her head forward. "That Arukia is sleeping on the Wozen's nest. I don't want even to think what Queen Neferteri would do to her—"

	"What else? That can't be the only thing." Laura kept firmly staring.

	"Sanbi-eye was wearing fake skin, and the old guy wearing fake skin named Sinoi is not the only one working for Queen Neferteri." Emelia looked at the ceiling, searching for answers. "Remember when I told you before, Arukia and Jugy worked together trying to find out where Gabiro was going, but they could never trace him after he left the castle gates? The same people could be setting up a trap for us\! That's why I told Arukia to be extra careful and not attempt confrontation at all costs."

	"So you brought Sinoi for extraction?" Laura sipped her tea, appreciating the aromatic flavors.

	Emelia nodded softly. "That's how we found out the hideout\! I told Arukia to bring the actual Sanbi-eye, as he directly correlates to the soul explosion on the 14th. Plus whatever journals or Nari Gels' experiments she can find."

	"Brain flush Sinoi, and for the other guy, if we suspect anything of interest with his body or soul, we can sell him to a scientist I know. Same for the nari gels." Laura continued the direct speech, "The Sanbi operation was a good income stream despite the hefty initial investment."

	Tears formed in Emelia's eyes. "But what if Sinoi doesn't survive it? Not like he's harming us\!"

Laura put her tea aside, moved the tea jug aside, and extended her hands towards Emelia. "It is what it is\! We protect ourselves first and foremost."

	Emelia looked back at the straight-faced Laura, now holding each other's hands atop the table.

	"I know you have the best intentions on this, but you acted too soon\!" Laura caressed her hands, "I know you want to protect them from harm, but they can defend themselves. They signed the contract to work for me just like they did for you. You trained them just like I trained you. You need to trust them more. Arukia played it smart by asking for PTO first\! You should have known that\!"

	"Except it didn't work\! Forway guards showed up at my door back on the 81st\! Nearly gave me a heart attack\! They were drilling me with questions about Arukia disappearing on her way to meet her parents. They even went looking around the shop\!" Emelia cleaned the forming tears again. "They finally eased up when I said she was my secret lover."

	"I see." Laura let go of her hands. "Review with Arukia why that happened. Do you remember the guard's name?"

	Emelia nodded softly.

	"Restart the Sanbi operations, but now hooking on the Sanbi captain instead of Sanbi Castle. Won't be as effective in gathering intel, but we must push ahead, or someone else will fill in the void." Laura spoke firmly as usual, taking a final sip of her tea and standing up. "I imagine they will cast a search quest on her, and likely sanbi-eye too. If the merchandise gets too hot, bury him in the woods and move on."

	"Poor guy\! What about King Itagiba? Why can't we extract info from the source itself? I know he drunkenly stumbled into our courtesans a few times already\!" Emelia smiled for the first time, "He's even spilled some stuff already\! It’s right there for the taking\!"

	"You know the answer to this\!" Laura had a slight head tilt, looking a bit disappointed and down at Emelia as she stood up.

	Emelia nodded, speaking from memory, reciting word for word verbatim as it was instructed to her a long ago. "We're in the business of gathering and selling information, not making enemies. Black badges always spat with one another, but turn that spat into an actual brawl, and we will get punched down\! What about Arukia? She's too hot for Sanbi right now."

	"Send her to Sweetlake and have her establish a new operation there. Flatland operation discovered the company behind Fii Water is planning to do some sort of expansion." Laura pushed the chair into the table, leaving it neatly flush against it. "Once the operation is set, Arukia will start to report directly to me."

	"Really?" Emelia stood up with bright eyes. "That's an amazing opportunity for her\! Thank you, madam\!"

	"Arukia proved herself capable." Laura nodded. "And so did you for turning the Sanbi operation profitable despite its challenges."
